OVERALL CONCLUSIONS
================================================================================

1. GENDER-SPECIFIC VULNERABILITIES:
   - Males: Meta-control deficits (perseveration, lapses, variability)
   - Females: Interference/cascade deficits (incongruent acc, error propagation)

2. LONELINESS EFFECTS ARE CONTEXT-DEPENDENT:
   - Main effects disappear after DASS control
   - Moderation by anxiety/stress levels
   - Strongest in younger males (developmental window)

3. TRIAL-LEVEL MECHANISMS:
   - Error cascade rates are low but systematic
   - Post-error slowing present but not consistently adaptive
   - Gender × UCLA interactions emerge at trial level

4. LATENT STRUCTURE:
   - Meta-control factor unifies 3 EF tasks
   - UCLA → Meta-Control → PE mediation plausible
   - Gender differences in factor structure/loadings

5. METHODOLOGICAL ADVANCES:
   - Composite indices increase interpretability
   - Trial-level GLMM captures dynamic processes
   - Commonality analysis clarifies unique vs shared variance
   - Age GAM reveals sensitive periods
   - SEM/PCA reduces measurement error
