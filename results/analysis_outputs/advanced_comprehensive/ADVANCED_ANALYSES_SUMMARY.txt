--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
7.5 RESOLUTION: MODERATION ≠ MAIN EFFECT
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

The inconsistency reflects DIFFERENT ANALYTICAL TARGETS, not contradictory findings:

Paper 1 Captures: CONDITIONAL SLOPES (gender moderation)
  - How UCLA predicts tau WITHIN each gender
  - Males: steep slope (r=0.566)
  - Females: flat slope (r=-0.078)

Paper 3 Captures: MARGINAL MEANS (profile membership)
  - Average tau levels across participants
  - Who has high tau on average?

CONCRETE EXAMPLE:

  Lonely Males (UCLA=50):
    Predicted tau = 150 + 0.566×(50-40) = 155.7 ms (HIGH)

  Non-Lonely Males (UCLA=30):
    Predicted tau = 150 + 0.566×(30-40) = 144.3 ms (LOW)

  Lonely Females (UCLA=50):
    Predicted tau = 150 - 0.078×(50-40) = 149.2 ms (MEDIUM)

  Non-Lonely Females (UCLA=30):
    Predicted tau = 150 - 0.078×(30-40) = 150.8 ms (MEDIUM)

  → High-tau profile: Lonely males + ALL females (mixed) → FEMALE-PREDOMINANT
  → Low-tau profile: Non-lonely males → MALE-PREDOMINANT

KEY INSIGHT:
  - Males show HIGH WITHIN-GENDER VARIABILITY (depends on UCLA)
  - Females show LOW WITHIN-GENDER VARIABILITY (independent of UCLA)
  - Profile analysis mixes lonely + non-lonely cases → masks moderation

BOTH PAPERS ARE CORRECT - they address different questions:
  - Paper 1: Does UCLA predict tau? → YES, for males only (moderation)
  - Paper 3: Are there subgroups? → YES, 2 profiles (classification)
