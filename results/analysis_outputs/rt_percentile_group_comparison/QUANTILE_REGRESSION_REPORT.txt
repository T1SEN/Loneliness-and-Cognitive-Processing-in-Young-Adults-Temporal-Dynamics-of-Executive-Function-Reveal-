================================================================================
QUANTILE REGRESSION ANALYSIS REPORT
================================================================================

PURPOSE
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Test whether UCLA × Gender effects are concentrated in RT distribution tails
(90th percentile = slow lapses) rather than central tendency (median).

HYPOTHESIS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
If loneliness affects attentional lapses (tau in Ex-Gaussian), we expect:
  - STRONG effects at q=0.90 (slow tail)
  - WEAK effects at q=0.50 (median)
This would converge with Ex-Gaussian tau findings.

METHOD
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Quantiles: [0.1, 0.25, 0.5, 0.75, 0.9]
Models tested: 10
Formula: RT(quantile) ~ UCLA × Gender + DASS(3) + Age

KEY FINDINGS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

WCST:
  overall:
    q=0.10: β=0.1737, p=0.4635 
    q=0.25: β=0.3140, p=0.1754 
    q=0.50: β=0.4448, p=0.0562 
    q=0.75: β=0.4530, p=0.0543 
    q=0.90: β=0.2863, p=0.2389 

Stroop:
  incongruent:
    q=0.10: β=0.4936, p=0.0315 *
    q=0.25: β=0.4319, p=0.0643 
    q=0.50: β=0.4284, p=0.0803 
    q=0.75: β=0.4762, p=0.0522 
    q=0.90: β=0.4732, p=0.0514 

INTERPRETATION
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
✗ Only 1/2 tasks show stronger tail effects
  → Effects NOT primarily driven by lapses
  → May reflect general slowing (mu) rather than tail (tau)

THEORETICAL IMPLICATIONS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
If q=0.90 >> q=0.50:
  → Loneliness × Gender affects LAPSES/attentional failures (not sustained slowing)
  → Supports reactive control deficit (occasional breakdowns)
  → Aligns with dual mechanisms of control (DMC) framework

If q=0.90 ≈ q=0.50:
  → Loneliness × Gender affects ENTIRE RT distribution uniformly
  → Supports general slowing (mu/sigma) rather than lapse-specific (tau)
  → May reflect energetic/motivational depletion

NEXT STEPS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
- Compare with Ex-Gaussian tau results for convergent validity
- If tail-dominant: Emphasize lapse-vulnerability in Discussion
- If uniform: Emphasize general slowing mechanism
