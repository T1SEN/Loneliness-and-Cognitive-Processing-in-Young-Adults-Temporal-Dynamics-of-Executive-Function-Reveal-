================================================================================
STROOP CONGRUENCY SEQUENCE EFFECT (CSE) - CONFLICT ADAPTATION ANALYSIS
================================================================================

OBJECTIVE
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Test whether lonely individuals (especially males) show impaired trial-to-trial
cognitive control adjustments using the Congruency Sequence Effect (CSE).

BACKGROUND
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
CSE (Gratton et al., 1992) measures dynamic conflict adaptation:
- Negative CSE = Reduced interference after incongruent trials (good adaptation)
- CSE ≈ 0 = No adaptation, flat interference regardless of N-1 trial
- Positive CSE = Increased interference after incongruent trials (maladaptive)

CSE Formula: (cI - cC) - (iI - iC)
  where:
    cI = RT incongruent trial after congruent trial
    cC = RT congruent trial after congruent trial
    iI = RT incongruent trial after incongruent trial
    iC = RT congruent trial after incongruent trial

SAMPLE
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Total N = 72
  Males: 27
  Females: 45

OVERALL CSE PATTERN
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Mean CSE = 9.8 ms (SD = 131.2)

✗ Positive CSE indicates maladaptive pattern (unusual)

GENDER DIFFERENCES IN CSE
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Males: M = 7.2 ms (SD = 136.4)
Females: M = 11.3 ms (SD = 129.5)
Independent t-test: t = -0.13, p = 0.8998

~ No significant gender difference in baseline CSE

UCLA → CSE CORRELATIONS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Overall: r = -0.031, p = 0.7977
Males (N=27): r = -0.223, p = 0.2633
  ~ No significant UCLA → CSE relationship in males
Females (N=45): r = 0.085, p = 0.5796
  ~ No significant UCLA → CSE relationship in females

INTERACTION MODEL: CSE ~ UCLA × Gender
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
UCLA main effect: β = 10.76, p = 0.5840
Gender main effect: β = -3.74, p = 0.9081
UCLA × Gender: β = -41.71, p = 0.2086
Model R² = 0.024

~ No significant UCLA × Gender interaction
  Loneliness → CSE relationship similar across genders

INTERPRETATION
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
FINDING: No significant loneliness → CSE relationship detected.

Theoretical implications:
- CSE tests PROACTIVE control (anticipatory strategy adjustment)
- Distinct from REACTIVE control (mean interference)
- Impaired CSE suggests reduced cognitive flexibility
- Can explain null mean Stroop findings if baseline is low

LIMITATIONS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
1. CSE requires sufficient trials in all 4 cells (cC, cI, iC, iI)
2. Practice effects may reduce CSE magnitude over task duration
3. Assumes stable conflict adaptation strategy across all trials
4. Does not distinguish proactive from reactive control mechanisms
