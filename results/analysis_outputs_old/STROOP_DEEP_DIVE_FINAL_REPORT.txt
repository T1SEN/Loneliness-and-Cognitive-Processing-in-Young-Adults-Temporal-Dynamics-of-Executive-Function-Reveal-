EXECUTIVE SUMMARY
================================================================================

After exhaustive analysis using multiple approaches, Stroop shows:
✅ ONE MARGINAL EFFECT: Quartile × UCLA (p=0.095)
❌ ALL OTHER ANALYSES: Non-significant (p > 0.15)

This contrasts sharply with:
- WCST: 13+ significant effects (p < 0.05), gender moderation p=0.004
- PRP: 13 significant effects including Ex-Gaussian τ r=0.578, p=0.002

================================================================================
ANALYSES PERFORMED
================================================================================

Phase 1-1: NEUTRAL BASELINE RE-ANALYSIS
----------------------------------------
Hypothesis: Traditional interference (Incong - Cong) obscures effects because
            congruent trials include facilitation

Methods:
- Pure Interference = Incongruent RT - Neutral RT
- Facilitation = Neutral RT - Congruent RT
- Tested UCLA × Gender moderation

Results (N=84):
  Metric                  UCLA×Gender β    p-value    Conclusion
  ----------------------  ---------------  ---------  -----------
  Pure Interference       -25.747          0.167      NS
  Facilitation            +21.984          0.163      NS
  Traditional             -3.763           0.856      NS

Gender-Stratified Correlations:
  Males:
    - Pure Interference: r=0.172, p=0.339
    - Facilitation: r=-0.256, p=0.150
    - Traditional: r=0.004, p=0.981

  Females:
    - Pure Interference: r=-0.084, p=0.558
    - Facilitation: r=0.104, p=0.466
    - Traditional: r=0.010, p=0.947

Conclusion: Neutral baseline does NOT reveal hidden effects.

---

Phase 1-2: BLOCK FATIGUE ANALYSIS
----------------------------------
Hypothesis: Effects emerge in late session (like WCST's 3.14× late effect)

Methods:
- Divided trials into quartiles (Q1-Q4)
- Calculated interference per quartile
- Tested Quartile × UCLA × Gender

Results (N=352 quartile observations from 88 participants):
  Effect                  p-value    Conclusion
  ----------------------  ---------  -----------
  Quartile × UCLA         0.095      MARGINAL ⭐
  Quartile × Gender       0.746      NS

Interpretation:
- As session progresses, UCLA's relationship with interference INCREASES
- Similar to WCST fatigue/depletion mechanism
- Effect size too small to reach significance with current N

Conclusion: Weak evidence for session-dependent effects, needs larger sample.

---

Phase 1-3: TIMEOUT/SLOW TRIAL ANALYSIS
---------------------------------------
Hypothesis: Loneliness affects attentional lapses (slow/timeout trials)

Methods:
- Slow trials defined as RT > 2000ms
- Calculated slow trial rate per condition × participant
- Tested UCLA × Gender moderation

Results (N=88):
  Metric                       UCLA      UCLA×Gender    Conclusion
  ---------------------------  --------  -------------  -----------
  Incongruent Slow Rate        p=0.743   p=0.894        NS

Conclusion: Loneliness does NOT predict attentional lapses in Stroop.
            Contrasts with PRP where τ (lapses) r=0.578, p=0.002.

---

Phase 1-4: RATIO INTERFERENCE
------------------------------
Hypothesis: Ratio (Incong/Cong) more sensitive than difference

Methods:
- Calculated RT ratio = Incongruent RT / Congruent RT
- Also tested log(ratio)
- Tested UCLA × Gender moderation

Results (N=88):
  Metric        Mean    UCLA      UCLA×Gender    Conclusion
  -----------   -----   --------  -------------  -----------
  Ratio         1.163   p=0.838   p=0.974        NS
  Log Ratio     -       p=0.820   p=0.969        NS

Conclusion: Ratio transformation does NOT increase sensitivity.

================================================================================
WHY STROOP SHOWS NULL EFFECTS: COMPARATIVE ANALYSIS
================================================================================

TASK COMPARISON TABLE
---------------------
| Feature              | WCST             | PRP                | Stroop          |
|----------------------|------------------|--------------------|--------------------|
| **Primary Effect**   | PE rate          | T2 RT variability  | Interference       |
| **Effect Size**      | β=2.59, p=0.004  | r=0.519, p=0.006   | β=-25.7, p=0.167   |
| **Parametric Var**   | PE vs NPE        | SOA (3 levels)     | Cong vs Incong (2) |
| **Ceiling Effect**   | No (10-20% PE)   | No (RT variability)| YES (98.7% acc)    |
| **Task Complexity**  | High (rule shift)| High (dual-task)   | LOW (color naming) |
| **Diagnostic Value** | High (PE=failure)| High (coordination)| LOW (automatic)    |

KEY DIFFERENCES:

1. **CEILING EFFECT**
   - Stroop: 98.7% accuracy → errors too rare for analysis
   - WCST/PRP: Lower accuracy allows error-based metrics

2. **TASK AUTOMATICITY**
   - Stroop: Highly automatic, practiced skill
   - WCST/PRP: Novel, cognitively demanding

3. **INDIVIDUAL DIFFERENCES**
   - Stroop interference: Small range (mean=136ms, SD=95ms)
   - WCST PE rate: Large range (mean=10%, SD=4%, max=23%)
   - PRP bottleneck: Large range (τ shows huge individual variability)

4. **PARAMETRIC MANIPULATION**
   - Stroop: Binary (Cong vs Incong)
   - PRP: Continuous (SOA gradient) → reveals graded differences
   - WCST: Multiple error types (PE, NPE, switch cost)

5. **COGNITIVE LOAD**
   - Stroop: Minimal (single-task, automatic)
   - PRP: High (dual-task coordination)
   - WCST: High (rule learning, set-shifting)

THEORETICAL INTERPRETATION
---------------------------
Loneliness effects on cognition may require:
✓ High cognitive load (to reveal resource depletion)
✓ Novel/complex tasks (not automatic)
✓ Sufficient error variance (diagnostic failures)

Stroop FAILS on all three criteria:
❌ Low load (automatic processing)
❌ Highly practiced (color naming)
❌ Ceiling effect (too easy)
