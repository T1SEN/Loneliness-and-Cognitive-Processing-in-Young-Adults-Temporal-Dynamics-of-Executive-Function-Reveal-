SECTION 5: SUMMARY & MANUSCRIPT IMPLICATIONS
================================================================================

PRIMARY CONCLUSION:
  Male-specific vulnerability to loneliness effects on cognitive flexibility
  Manifested specifically in WCST perseverative errors and accuracy
  NOT found in Stroop (interference control) or PRP (dual-task)

MECHANISM INSIGHTS:
  1. DIRECT effect (not mediated by DASS)
  2. CONTEXT-DEPENDENT (moderated by psychopathology level)
  3. PROCESS-SPECIFIC (rule-switching recovery, error chains)

STATISTICAL ROBUSTNESS:
  ✓ Permutation tests confirm effects (p=0.005-0.007)
  ✓ Bootstrap CIs exclude zero
  ✓ Outlier-resistant
  ✗ Does NOT survive Bonferroni correction (exploratory finding)

NOVEL CONTRIBUTIONS:
  1. First evidence of gender-moderated loneliness-EF relationship
  2. Trial-level mechanism (post-shift errors, error chains)
  3. DASS stratification reveals boundary conditions
  4. Domain-specificity (WCST only, not Stroop/PRP)

LIMITATIONS:
  1. Small male sample (N=27) → underpowered
  2. Multiple testing → exploratory status
  3. Cross-sectional → cannot infer causality
  4. College sample → limited generalizability

RECOMMENDED NEXT STEPS:
  1. IMMEDIATE: Preregistered replication (N≥150, balanced gender)
  2. SHORT-TERM: Add rumination/coping measures to test mechanisms
  3. LONG-TERM: Longitudinal design + intervention studies
